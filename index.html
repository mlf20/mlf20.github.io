About

I am a PhD student at Imperial College London under the supervision of Dr. Sergio Maffeis. My research focus is on applying Reinforcement Learning to Web Application Security to find vulnerabilities. Prior to this I received a Masters of Engineering from University College London, earning the ‘Outstanding MEng Graduating Student’ prize from the Department of Electronic and Electrical Engineering.

Education and Experience

Education

Doctorate of Philosophy

2020 - Present

Department of Computing, Imperial College London

My research focus in my PhD is on developing Reinforcement Learning applications to improve the security of web applications and Networks. Specifially for finding vulnerabilities in webapplications (e.g. XSS, SQLi), access control vulnerabilities in web APIs, and autonomous network defence. This work involved the experimentation and implementation of a variety of deep Reinforcement Learning techniques in addition to meta learning, hierarchical learning, and curiosity.

Masters of Engineering

2016 - 2020

Electronic Engineering with Computer Science, University College London

My undergraduate project focused on the design and implementation of a graph-deep-Q-network in Tensorflow, a novel neural network of my own design. This model was applied to the problem of packet routing, applying Reinforcement Learning to the non-Euclidean domain of graph structures.

In my Masters thesis I coordinated a team on a computer vision task. This project developed an advanced driver assistance system (ADAS) capable of predicting lane changes of vehicles in front of a car. I was the project lead responsible for ensuring the project stays on task. I oversaw the data processing on this task, in addition to the implementation of trajectory prediction algorithm, and model hyper parameter tuning.

Project Collaborator

November 2021 - Present

Alan Turing Institute

Conducting research with a group of leading academics at the Turing has allowed me to extend my research activities beyond the scope of my PhD. This has involved developing techniques to combat the large state space when applying reinforcement learning to practical applications. Our Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning model went on to win the First CAGE Challenge this international challenge has agents defend a small network against multiple classes of advanced persistent threat. You can read about it in our paper that won the best poster award at AsiaCCS '22 here or page 19 of the Alan Turing Institute Annual Report 2021-2022.
